# Policy Document Skill

Draft and review HR policies and internal documents for Talosix. Policies must be compliant with applicable employment regulations, reflect company values, and be written in clear, accessible language that all employees can understand.

## When to Use

- Drafting a new company policy
- Updating an existing policy for compliance changes
- Reviewing a policy draft for completeness and clarity
- Creating internal procedural documents
- Standardizing policy format across the organization

## Policy Categories and Compliance Requirements

### Employment Policies
- **At-will employment**: State clearly (where applicable by jurisdiction)
- **Equal Employment Opportunity**: Required by Title VII, ADA, ADEA
- **Anti-harassment and discrimination**: Required; include reporting procedures
- **Reasonable accommodations**: ADA compliance, interactive process
- **Leave policies**: FMLA, state-specific leave laws, parental leave
- **Compensation and pay practices**: FLSA compliance, equal pay

### Workplace Policies
- **Remote work / hybrid policy**: Equipment, expectations, security requirements
- **Code of conduct**: Professional behavior standards
- **Conflict of interest**: Disclosure requirements, clinical trial industry considerations
- **Social media**: Guidelines for representing the company
- **Dress code**: If applicable, inclusive and reasonable

### Information Security and Data Protection
- **Acceptable use**: IT systems, email, internet
- **Data classification and handling**: Especially important for clinical trial data
- **Confidentiality**: NDA reinforcement, PHI/PII handling
- **Bring your own device (BYOD)**: If applicable
- **Incident reporting**: Security and data breach procedures
- **Record retention**: Regulatory requirements for GxP records

### Health and Safety
- **Workplace safety**: OSHA compliance
- **Drug-free workplace**: Policy and testing (if applicable)
- **Workers' compensation**: Reporting procedures
- **Ergonomics**: Especially for remote workers

### Regulated Industry-Specific Policies
- **Training policy**: GxP training requirements, training documentation
- **SOP compliance**: Requirement to follow Standard Operating Procedures
- **Change control participation**: Employee responsibilities in change management
- **Regulatory inspection readiness**: Employee obligations during audits/inspections
- **Whistleblower protection**: Reporting regulatory concerns without retaliation
- **Conflict of interest with clinical trials**: No personal participation in trials for products Talosix supports

## Writing Guidelines

### Clarity and Accessibility

1. **Write at an 8th-grade reading level** where possible
   - Short sentences (under 25 words average)
   - Common words over jargon
   - Active voice over passive voice

2. **Use clear structure**
   - Numbered sections for easy reference
   - Bullet points for lists
   - Tables for complex information
   - Headers that describe content

3. **Be specific, not vague**
   - "Submit within 5 business days" not "Submit promptly"
   - "Contact HR at hr@talosix.com" not "Contact the appropriate department"
   - "Three occurrences within 12 months" not "Repeated violations"

4. **Avoid legalese where possible**
   - "You must" not "Employees shall be required to"
   - "This policy covers" not "The scope of this policy encompasses"
   - "If you break this policy" not "In the event of non-compliance with this policy"

5. **Include examples** where the policy might be ambiguous
   - "Conflict of interest examples include: a family member working at a competing EDC company, holding stock in a sponsor whose trials we support, or accepting gifts from vendors."

### Cultural Alignment

Talosix policies should reflect:
- **Trust**: Default to trusting employees; policies guide rather than control
- **Transparency**: Explain the "why" behind policies, not just the "what"
- **Inclusivity**: Consider diverse employee situations (parents, caregivers, different abilities, different locations)
- **Innovation**: Policies should enable work, not create unnecessary bureaucracy
- **Integrity**: Especially important in regulated clinical trial industry

### Legal Compliance Checklist

Before finalizing any policy, verify:
- [ ] Compliant with federal law (FLSA, FMLA, ADA, Title VII, OSHA, etc.)
- [ ] Compliant with applicable state laws (which may be more protective)
- [ ] Compliant with local ordinances (city/county requirements)
- [ ] Non-discriminatory in language and application
- [ ] Reasonable accommodations addressed where applicable
- [ ] Consistent with existing policies (no contradictions)
- [ ] Reviewed by legal counsel (recommended for all employment policies)
- [ ] Includes required notices and disclosures
- [ ] Distribution and acknowledgment process defined

**Important**: Always recommend legal review before policy adoption. Generated policies are drafts requiring professional legal review for the specific jurisdictions in which Talosix operates.

## Controlled Document Requirements

As a GxP company, Talosix policies may be controlled documents:

- **Document numbering**: Consistent ID scheme (POL-HR-001, POL-IT-002)
- **Version control**: Track all revisions with change descriptions
- **Review cycle**: Annual review at minimum, triggered review for regulatory changes
- **Approval workflow**: Draft -> Review -> Approve -> Distribute -> Acknowledge
- **Training**: Determine if policy changes require employee re-training
- **Retention**: Retain superseded versions per retention schedule
- **Access**: Current versions easily accessible to all applicable employees

## Output Format

Generate policy documents in clean markdown using the standard template. Include all sections. Mark sections that require company-specific input with `[COMPANY INPUT NEEDED: <what is needed>]`. Always include the disclaimer that legal review is recommended before adoption.
