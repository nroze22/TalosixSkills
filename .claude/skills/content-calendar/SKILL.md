# Content Calendar Planning for Talosix EDC

## Purpose

Create structured content calendars that coordinate Talosix marketing efforts across all channels, align with clinical trial industry events and cycles, and ensure consistent thought leadership throughout the year. The calendar serves as the single source of truth for marketing content planning.

## Annual Planning Framework

### Industry Event Calendar (Core Anchor Dates)

Plan content around these major clinical trial industry events each year. Exact dates shift annually — confirm 3-4 months ahead.

**Q1 (January - March)**
| Event | Typical Timing | Content Opportunity |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| SCOPE Summit | Feb | Site operations, outsourcing, CRO content |
| ACRP Conference | variable | Site management, CRA training, monitoring |
| FDA/EMA guidance releases | Ongoing | Regulatory commentary and analysis |

**Q2 (April - June)**
| Event | Typical Timing | Content Opportunity |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| DIA Global Annual Meeting | June | Broadest industry audience; regulatory + data science |
| SCDM (if spring event) | variable | Data management, CDISC, data quality |
| Clinical trial awareness periods | variable | Patient-centric content, public interest angles |

**Q3 (July - September)**
| Event | Typical Timing | Content Opportunity |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| Summer slowdown (reduced engagement) | Jul-Aug | Evergreen content, list posts, lighter formats |
| ACRP regional events | variable | Site-focused content |
| Back-to-business push | Sep | New product launches, fall campaign kickoffs |

**Q4 (October - December)**
| Event | Typical Timing | Content Opportunity |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| SCDM Annual Conference | Oct | Data management, CDISC, quality metrics |
| DIA Europe | variable | European regulatory, EU CTR content |
| Year-end / predictions season | Dec | Year in review, trends and predictions for next year |
| Budget planning season | Oct-Dec | ROI content, cost comparison, business case content |

### Industry Cycle Alignment

Beyond events, align content with the natural rhythms of clinical trial operations:

- **Q1:** New budgets allocated — decision-makers evaluating new vendors. Push bottom-funnel content (demos, comparisons, ROI calculators).
- **Q2:** Studies launching from Q1 planning. Emphasize implementation speed and study build content.
- **Q3:** Mid-year reviews and compliance audits. Push data quality, compliance, and audit readiness content.
- **Q4:** Planning for next year. Push strategic content, trend reports, and business case materials.

## Channel-Specific Cadence

### Blog
- **Frequency:** 2-3 posts per month
- **Mix:** 1 pillar/long-form, 1-2 standard posts
- **Lead time:** Draft due 2 weeks before publish date
- **SEO:** Each post targets a specific keyword from the SEO strategy

### Social Media (LinkedIn Primary)
- **Frequency:** 3-5 posts per week
- **Mix per week:**
  - 1 blog/content promotion
  - 1 industry commentary or trend insight
  - 1 product or feature highlight
  - 1 company culture, team, or event post
  - 1 engagement post (question, poll, or discussion prompt)
- **Lead time:** 1 week ahead, with flexibility for real-time commentary

### Email
- **Frequency:** 2-4 sends per month (excluding automated nurture sequences)
- **Mix:**
  - 1 newsletter (monthly roundup of content, product news, event)
  - 1 campaign email (aligned with monthly theme, gated content, webinar promo)
  - 1-2 targeted sends (segment-specific, event-triggered)
- **Lead time:** Draft 2 weeks ahead, review 1 week ahead, send schedule confirmed 3 days ahead

### Webinar
- **Frequency:** 1 per month (or bi-monthly during slower periods)
- **Planning lead time:** 6 weeks minimum
- **Alignment:** Topic matches monthly theme and supports a content offer
- **Promotion window:** 3-4 weeks pre-event across email and social

### White Paper / Gated Content
- **Frequency:** 1 per quarter
- **Planning lead time:** 8-10 weeks
- **Supports:** Quarterly campaign theme, lead generation goals
- **Promotion:** Dedicated email, social series, blog post supporting the topic

### Case Study
- **Frequency:** 1-2 per quarter (dependent on customer availability)
- **Pipeline:** Maintain a backlog of 3-5 candidate customers at all times
- **Approval timeline:** Allow 4-6 weeks for customer review and sign-off

### Press Release
- **Frequency:** As needed (product launches, partnerships, milestones)
- **Planning:** Align major announcements with events or theme months where possible
- **Lead time:** 2-3 weeks for drafting and approval

## Cross-Channel Coordination Rules

### Content Atomization
Every major content asset should be atomized across channels:

**White paper produces:**
- 2-3 blog posts (excerpted/adapted sections)
- 5-10 social media posts (key stats, quotes, frameworks)
- 2 email sends (announcement + follow-up)
- 1 webinar (deep dive on the paper's topic)
- Paid ad creative (promote the gated download)

**Webinar produces:**
- 1 blog post (key takeaways summary)
- Recording available as on-demand gated content
- 3-5 social posts (highlights, quotes, key slides)
- 1 email to non-attendees with recording
- Slides repurposed as SlideShare or carousel

**Case study produces:**
- 1 blog post (narrative version or supporting context)
- 3-5 social posts (key metrics, customer quote)
- Email inclusion in nurture sequences
- Sales enablement one-pager
- Conference presentation material

### Channel Sequencing for Launches
When launching a major piece of content or announcement:

| Day | Channel | Action |
|-----|---------|--------|
| Day -7 | Social | Teaser posts building anticipation |
| Day -3 | Email | Early access or preview to VIP list |
| Day 0 | All | Full launch: blog, email, social, PR (if applicable) |
| Day +1 | Social | Behind-the-scenes, key takeaway posts |
| Day +3 | Email | Follow-up to non-openers, additional angle |
| Day +7 | Social | User reactions, additional data points |
| Day +14 | Blog | Related post linking back to primary asset |
| Day +30 | Email | Include in monthly newsletter roundup |

## Planning Process

### Quarterly Planning (6 Weeks Before Quarter Start)
1. Review previous quarter performance metrics
2. Identify upcoming industry events and key dates
3. Set quarterly MQL and pipeline contribution targets
4. Select quarterly theme arc and primary campaign
5. Plan gated content asset and webinar topics
6. Align with product roadmap for launch timing
7. Draft quarterly content calendar at monthly-theme level

### Monthly Planning (2 Weeks Before Month Start)
1. Confirm monthly theme and key topics
2. Assign specific blog post titles, keywords, and authors
3. Draft social media content plan by week
4. Finalize email send schedule and segments
5. Confirm webinar details (topic, speakers, date)
6. Identify ad campaigns launching or adjusting
7. Populate weekly calendar with all deliverables and deadlines

### Weekly Standup (Monday)
1. Review this week's publishing schedule
2. Confirm all content is on track (drafted, reviewed, approved)
3. Identify gaps or delays and adjust
4. Review previous week's performance highlights
5. Flag any reactive content opportunities (industry news, competitor moves)

## Output Deliverables

When creating a content calendar, provide:
1. Annual theme map with monthly themes and rationale
2. Quarterly plan with campaign focus, gated content, and targets
3. Monthly calendar in the template format above (all channels populated)
4. Content atomization plan for each major asset
5. Industry event alignment notes
6. Recommended metrics dashboard structure for tracking performance
